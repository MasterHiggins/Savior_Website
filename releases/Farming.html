Farming

Article from Chris Higgins
(Developer)
This article is focused on Part 1 and has Farming spoilers

Farming is a great way to gain materials. After finishing the mountains, you'll be able to go back to the island to farm. Because of the great ending fooling up local ecosystems, you're growing literal material.

These crops will take roughly 10 minutes to grow at first. However, you can offer materials to a statue on the farm to speed this process up.

There's also a few more statues. Interact with these to get certain boosts. You can move faster, you can deal more damage, lose the spell cooldown, lower spell costs, etc. Of course, you can also use these materials to upgrade your main weapon as well.

Farming is intended to get many upgrades in the second part. Originally, there weren't going to be any trades, but it didn't feel like it would be worth it if that were the case, so I went this route instead. You can get all your levels up first.

To harvest your materials, you need to offer a statue of harvesting 250 materials. This decreases 10 times everytime you harvest until it hits 0. The farm size is 400 tiles, and each crop produces 0-2 materials. That's 0-800 Materials for 250, but free after 25 harvests. Crops will only grow when you are present. It's a 25% chance of 0 materials, then a 75% chance of 1 material and a 25% chance of 2. So a 25% chance of a dud, or 75% chance of materials.

Below is a list of farming trades available. Please note these can change.

Decrease growth time by 5%, up to a total of 50% faster (250 Materials)
Increase your speed by 5% (300 Materials)
Increase your total damage dealt by 5 points (575 Materials)
Gain 1 health, up to 5 times (750 Materials)
Reduce the Spell countdown to 5 seconds (1000 Materials)
Reduce Spell Costs by 25%, up to 50% (800 Materials)
Get a 'sun-shard' (2500 Materials)
Increase material gain from enemies by 10% up to 50% (300 Materials)
Unlock a new spell (750 Materials)
Waste materials (10,000 Materials)
Auto Harvest, unlocked after harvest price is 0 (1,250 Materials)
Increase max materials by 25%, up to 200% (250 Materials)
Have spells cost materials, not health (500 Materials)
Have spells cost health, not materials (500 Materials)
Enemy attacks have a 5% chance to miss, up to 20% (250 Materials)
Extend the size of your sword by 10% (300 Materials)
Chests can yeild up to 3 extra materials (150 Materials)
Double all eligible stats, regardless of limits, after getting everything (20,000 Materials)
Gamble for a 50/50 chance of getting materials (10 Materials)

For gambling, here is additional stats, which can only happen after getting the 50%:

35% chance of getting 10 Materials
25% chance of getting 50 Materials
20% chance of getting 100 Materials
10% chance of getting 175 Materials
9% chance of getting 200 Materials
1% chance of getting 500 Materials

For that last 1% chance, the game then rolls for a 50% chance. If it gets that instead, you will get 1000 Materials. Unless... The game rolls it again. That 1% chance, then 50%, and then another roll. If you get a 1% chance, you'll get 5,000 Materials. Now, this is incredible unlikely, a 1 in 40,000, or 0.0025% chance, but it could happen. Your odds of gaining are actually considerably high considering that it's pretty likely you could get a 500 Material payout early.

I also want to touch on the doubling of all stats. For this, you need max growth speed reduced, your speed increased, 5 extra health from farming, max spell cost reduction, the sun shard, max material gain from enemies, the new spell, wasted materials, auto harvest, max material capacity and max enemy miss chance. That's a lot to get, but then your stats can finally be doubled.

All statues also has a refund sister statue. If you interact with that statue, then you'll get the invested materials back, except for gambling, which does not have a refund statue. Instead, there is a statue that adds the chance of getting 5 Materials, changing the roll to 25% chance of nothing, 25% chance of 5 materials, and the rest as normal. This statues can activate it's perk for 100 Materials.

When your stats are doubled, you actually can't refund this statue. Instead, you can refund the affected doubled stats individually, and then their restrictions are doubled. For example, the maximum material capacity can be refunded, and then you can buy it up to 400% instead fo 200%. When refunded, it will give you the necessary amount to purchase back up to the doubled amount, not what you paid into it.

Farming is more in depth than it is more so farming. It's solid, but it's not farming like Stardew Valley for example. It's more like a tycoon, which I much prefer. It adds more background gameplay, or something to have on the side while watching a video.
